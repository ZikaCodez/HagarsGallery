Behind the Canvas 🎨✨

Ever wondered what goes on in an artist’s mind? Here’s a sneak peek into my creative process:

  • 🎨
    It starts out of nowhere: Most of my sketches happen spontaneously, especially when I’m drawing on paper. You’d find me doodling in the middle of studying just to clear my mind. That’s why most of my sketches are on lined paper, not sketchbooks!
  • ☕
    Setting the mood: When I’m working on digital art, I make sure to set the vibe. I’d make myself an iced coffee or grab a Red Bull (depending on the budget 😅). Then, I’d prepare everything, only to realize I’m stuck with an art block. That’s when I turn to Pinterest for some inspiration before I start.
  • 🎶
    Music is everything: My music taste has changed over the years, but back then, I was obsessed with Conan Gray—he’s the only constant. I’d also listen to artists like Keshi and SUGGI. Their music is calm, soothing, and perfect for getting into the zone. Sometimes I’d use headphones to fully immerse myself, and other times I’d play it out loud. But most of the time, I’d focus on the instruments and let the music guide my mood.
  • 🎨
    Color palette struggles: I didn’t have a fixed approach to colors. If I was drawing a character, I’d stick to their main colors. Otherwise, I’d just pick colors that looked good together and complemented each other. Back then, I didn’t understand color theory or shadows—I just went with what felt right. Let me tell you, color theory is a pain in the ass! I was clueless about it, so I didn’t follow any rules.
  • ⏳
    No breaks allowed: I never liked leaving a piece unfinished. Once I started, I had to finish it in one go. Stepping away would take me out of the mood I worked so hard to get into.

Art isn’t just about the final piece—it’s about the journey, the mood, and the little quirks that make it unique. Let’s create something unforgettable! 🚀✨
